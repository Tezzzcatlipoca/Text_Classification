Plague in Egypt. 

The Italian Government has suspended the special precautions taken 
against arrivals from Egypt on account of bubonic plague. 

Plague in Mauritius. 

A telegram from the governor of Mauritius announces that for the 
week ending May 14, 1903, there was one fatal case of plague on the 
island. 

Plague in Peru. 

The following official telegram from Lima, dated May 12, 1903, has 
been received by the Peruvian legation at London: 

"Four cases of bubonic plague have appeared in Callao, but no 

resh cases have broken out since the 7th instant. Lima and Ancon 

are both absolutely free from the plague. Ancon has meanwhile been 

declared a first-class commercial port, and takes the place of Callao 

till all danger disappears." 

JAPAN. 

Report from Yokohama. 

Assistant Surgeon Moore reports, May 6, as follows: During the 
week ended May 2, 1903, 4 vessels, having an aggregate personnel of 
414 crew and 923 passengers, were granted bills of health; 474 steer- 
age passengers were bathed and 640 pieces of baggage were disinfected. 

For the week ended April 25 there were reported in Yokohama 
infectious diseases as follows: Enteric fever, 3 cases, no deaths; diph- 
theria, 2 cases, no deaths.
